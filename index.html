About Me

Hello! I'm Mike Gao, currently in my second year as a BBA/BCS student (Business Administration and Computer Science at UW). As of right now, I'm diving into a diverse field of courses, which include Object-Oriented Software Development, Statistics and Probability, Financial Accounting, Financial Management, and Organizational behavior.


My journey goes beyond the classroom since I've developed my leadership and communication skills through past roles as a programming instructor. By working with organizations such as Canfly Education Canada and Cautiontape Programming, I instructed youth of a diverse array of ages about the foundations of programming (variables, conditionals, loops, syntax, etc.) using programming languages such as Java and Scratch. Through this experience, I learned how to explain complicated ideas in simple layman's terms, an important skill that I have taken with me to the present. I facilitated learning through IDES such as Eclipse and Raspberry Pi, dedicating significant time to lesson preparation and course content, coordinating with each organization's objectives.


Recently, I've been working on a game called "Blast" - a fast-paced puzzle game inspired by a very entertaining minigame from Activate Games. Using pygame, I explored a wide array of concepts such as object-oriented programming, particle effects, graphics, animations, and the principles of high cohesion and low coupling.


Aside from my education and programming, I pursue my hobbies in music, including piano performance, music composition, and music production. I embark on culinary adventures, anime, and participate in sports such as Volleyball and Skiing.


Overall, I have a strong passion for technology, innovation, and transforming my ideas into awesome creations. I would love to work with you - my contact information is below.
